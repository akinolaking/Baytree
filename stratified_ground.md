# Stratified Ground

The earth beneath infrastructure is never uniform — it is strata. Layers of compacted fill, engineered geosynthetic membranes, and natural geology stacked with deliberate purpose. This philosophy draws its visual grammar from that hidden cross-section: the view you see when you cut through a road, a seawall, or a reinforced embankment. Parallel horizontal layers of varying density and material — some dark and load-bearing, some translucent and filtering — create a visual rhythm that is both systematic and organic. The result must feel like an artifact from an engineering discipline: meticulously crafted, the product of deep expertise, labored over with painstaking attention until every proportion achieves inevitability.

Colour is geological. The dominant register moves between deep forest green — the colour of dense vegetation reclaiming a stabilised slope — and weathered concrete grey. These are not decorative choices; they are material references. Green signals the living surface; grey signals the engineered substrate beneath. Together they form a gradient system that transitions between states: natural to constructed, surface to foundation, visible to hidden. The accent — a single warm earth tone, the colour of laterite clay or sun-dried Nigerian sandstone — appears only at the point of maximum structural importance: the junction where the mark meets the word. The palette is three values maximum, each pulled from the physical world of the company's work, each applied with the restraint of a colourist who has spent countless hours calibrating relationships between adjacent hues.

The mark is not separate from the typography — it is embedded within it. The stacked horizontal bars from the existing identity are preserved but reimagined as precision-engineered strata: five parallel lines of calculated width, spacing, and tonal graduation that sit *inside* or *above* the wordmark as an integrated system. These bars reference geosynthetic layers, road cross-sections, and the horizontal datum lines of marine engineering drawings. Their graduation — from deep green through grey to light — encodes the company's expertise in layered infrastructure systems. The bars are the logo's fingerprint; no other engineering company has this motif. Master-level execution means each bar's weight, spacing, and colour value has been tested through dozens of iterations.

Typography is architectural — each letter a structural element with internal logic. The wordmark uses Lato Bold with deliberate tracking that creates modular spacing between glyphs. Where the existing logo placed a tree between T and R, the evolved mark places the strata system: the five bars sit at the junction point, integrated into the word itself as though the typography was built around them. The descriptor line in Lato Light sits beneath with the precision of an engineering drawing annotation. Every alignment is verified against an invisible 8-point grid. Nothing is decorative. Nothing is accidental. The composition tells you this company builds things that last, designed by people who measure twice and cut once.
